🧠 The Revision System (Remember It for Life)

Completing a course ≠ remembering it. These 3 layers are built into every week above so knowledge moves into long-term memory.

Layer 1 — Revision Sundays

  • Every Sunday: 1–1.5 hr recap of the week
  • Re-do key exercises without looking
  • Update your notes & flashcards
  • Re-explain one concept out loud (Feynman technique)

Layer 2 — Spaced Repetition

  • Revisit each topic after 1 day → 1 week → 1 month
  • Built into later weeks (e.g., AWS revisited in Week 5)
  • Use Anki or a simple flashcard sheet
  • Only re-study cards you got wrong

Layer 3 — Consolidation Phase

  • Weeks 11–12: full revision, no new content
  • Rebuild a project from memory
  • Re-take practice exams
  • Mock interviews = active recall under pressure
The retention rule: If you can't explain a concept out loud or solve a problem without notes, you haven't learned it yet — you've only seen it. Active recall > re-watching videos.
